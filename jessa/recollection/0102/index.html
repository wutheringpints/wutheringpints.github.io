She thinks to herself, looking into a nearby window, before pouting and looking away.

T.L: "Ah well, won't matter for long!"


The Lady looks towards a tall, abandoned looking building where she was informed "Shark" was staying. Though the windows were boarded up, the smell of rot lingered. She was quite familiar with this smell due to one of her ongoing lines of work. I suppose I should tell you know that The Lady you're reading about at this moment is in fact a fixer, this is important info as it allows us to understand her line of thinking. You see, this rotting smell that she's grown to know is sort of like the sun. What I mean by this, is that (in a relative sense) the sun always stays the same. However, the distance and our perception of it changes. In the same way, the smell of rot always has that horrible, sickening stench to it, however there are situations where certain aspects or scents of the rot change. This is to say that there are no two truly rotting smells. I believe I'm getting off track now so I will continue as I were.


She used this smell of rot as an indicator that someone was using this building, and she had the correct place. It indicated there was a death here recently. If there was no scent, it meant that someone had either died here a long time ago, or no one had died here at all. Regardless, she pressed on into the building.

This is where it became apparent to her that this area was within the Middle's territory, whether it had been the added smell of alcohol, the purple cloaks draped over old, dusty furniture, and those speakers, speakers she's grown to know as a threat of sorts, she's seen them so many times on so many jobs that she can't even remember the first time she saw them. They do mean one thing for her however: She's getting close. Dangerously close.
